Hola! Welcome to my personal website

I’m an information systems engineer (currently doing a public policy and management master) who loves to work with data. I believe in an Internet for good that serves people and communities, empowering them to unleash all their potential.

Here are some projects I've worked on:

  • Virtuagora
    Virtuagora is a citizen participation digital platform I co-funded and we deployed more than 10 instances for local governments and civil society organizations. Some examples are Ingenia, San Lorenzo's participatory budget, and Creating Networks.
  • Lyris IT
    I started at Lyris as developer and consultant, building digital transformation solutions based on documents management, public key infrastructures and digital signatures. Currently I'm its CTO and project manager of Signar, our flagship software.
  • Youth IGF Argentina 🇦🇷
    Youth Initiatives of the IGF are created with a goal of encouraging and involving young people in a substantive discussion on Internet governance. I was a founding member of the Youth Argentina and I'm currently an active committee member. I'm also maintaining its website.
  • Embajadores de Internet
    Embajadores is a non-profit initiative created by 2016 fellows of ISOC's Youth@IGF program. We aim to introduce people in the Internet governance ecosystem generating content in Spanish. You can check our website, which I created and I'm in charge of maintaining it.
  • LACNIC and FORT project
    ~ If you come from RDR this is my highlighted project! ~
    FORT is a joint initiative by LACNIC and NIC.MX to contribute to RPKI deployment and a more secure and resilient Internet. I joined FORT as a consultant in 2019 and I made its Diagnostic Report. Later, I continued doing research work with LACNIC related to BGP interconnection in the LAC region. You can read our report or take a look at the data toolkit I developed to extract data from global BGP collectors and get relevant data about the autonomous systems and countries in Latin America. Data is presented on this website too.
  • MANRS Fellow
    MANRS is a global initiative, supported by the Internet Society, that provides crucial fixes to reduce the most common routing threats. The Fellowship allows individuals to bring new perspectives, innovative ideas, and recent research experience into the MANRS work to improve routing security. As a fellow I posted the following articles “ A Regional Look into BGP Incidents in 2020” and “ What’s the AS0 ROA policy, and what should I know as a network operator?” on its blog.
